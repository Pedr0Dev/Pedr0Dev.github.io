06 tech trends for 2022 according to Gartner
More than a tradition, the trends released by Gartner are always a guide on what will be adopted in technology in the following year. And 2022 will be no different. This year, the list includes 06 strategic trends that will enable CEOs to fully embark on digital transformation, delivering growth and efficiency to their businesses. In addition to positioning CIOs and IT executives as strategic partners in the organization. Check out the technologies that will act as force multipliers for digital business and innovation in the next three to five years.
#1 Data Fabric
The value of data has never been clearer for digital business transformation. However, they often remain isolated within applications, meaning they are not being used as effectively as possible. According to Gartner, by 2024 the Data Fabric, or data mesh, will quadruple data utilization efficiency by reducing human information management tasks by 70%. This is because of its ability to use analytics to actively learn and recommend where data should be used and changed. It is a tool that offers flexible and resilient integration of data sources across platforms and business users, making them available wherever they are needed, regardless of where they are concentrated.
#2 Cybersecurity Loop
The cyber security fabric is one of the main and most important among the technological trends for the coming years. That's because it allows the best standalone security solutions to work together to improve overall protection. The technology has the ability to quickly and reliably verify identity, context, and policy adherence across cloud and non-cloud environments. In other words, it is a much more complete solution than traditional security tools, which, in addition to making organizations more vulnerable to cybercriminals, also do not offer a scalable environment. A cybersecurity fabric architecture protects all assets, regardless of location, to enable a security approach that spans the entire foundation of IT services. Gartner expects that by 2024, organizations that adopt the solution to integrate security tools so that they work as an ecosystem will reduce the financial impact of individual incidents by 90%.
#3 Privacy Enhancement Computing
The real value of data is not simply in having it, but in how it is used for AI models, analytics and insights. Privacy-enhancing computing approaches rightly allow information to be shared across ecosystems, creating value while preserving privacy. Processes vary but include encryption, splitting or pre-processing of sensitive data to allow it to be handled without compromising confidentiality.
#4 Cloud Native Platforms
Migrating to the cloud is one of the hottest technology trends. However, “lift-and-shift” cloud migrations – migrating applications and associated data to the cloud with minimal or no changes – require a lot of maintenance and do not enjoy any of the benefits of the cloud. Cloud-native platforms use elasticity and scalability to deliver more agility. In addition, they reduce dependency on an infrastructure, freeing up time to focus on application functionality. According to Gartner, by 2025, cloud-native platforms will serve as the foundation for more than 95% of new digital products.
#5 Composite Applications
Composite applications are built from modular, business-centric components. Its main objective is to facilitate the use and reuse of code, accelerating the time to market of new software solutions. In 2024, the mantra for new SaaS and custom apps will be to be “API-first or API-only composable”, rendering traditional SaaS and customizing apps as legacy.
#6 Decision Intelligence
In a world of rapid change, organizations must make better decisions faster. Decision intelligence can support and enhance human decision making and potentially automate it through the use of augmented analytics, simulations and AI. Gartner expects that by 2023, more than a third of large organizations will have analysts practicing decision intelligence.
