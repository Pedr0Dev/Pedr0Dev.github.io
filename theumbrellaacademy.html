The Umbrella Academy: Season 4 will be the last on Netflix.
Netflix confirmed this Thursday (25) that the hit series from the Umbrella Academy will be this fourth and final streaming. The news was welcomed by series creator Steve Blackman in a statement.
Maykon Lafaette 12/05/2019
The Umbrella Academy new season banner
“I was hoping that the incredibly loyal fans of The Umbrella Academy will be able to experience the fitting end to the Hargreeves brothers' journey that we began five years ago. But before we reach that conclusion, we have an incredible story ahead of us for Season 4 that will have fans 'chained' until the final minutes.” Said the series creator.
Netflix has confirmed the return of Elliot Page, Tom Hopper, David Castañeda, Emmy Raver-Lampman, Robert Sheehan, Aidan Gallagher, Justin H. Min, Ritu Arya, and Colm Feore for the new episodes. Additionally, Blackman will continue to run the story as showrunner. It is worth remembering that the story had already been planned to end in the fourth season, as Blackman himself commented in an interview with TVLine last year. "I think [four seasons] is the ideal conclusion to the series. Netflix might want more than that, but I have a sweet idea of ​​where I think the show should end and where we're headed," he said at the time.
American Executive Producer and Screenwriter Steve Blackman.
Based on the books of the same name by Gerard Way, the series follows the members of a family of superheroes as they investigate the mysterious death of their father and deal with their divergent personalities and abilities. The third season hit Netflix in June and ended with a hook for new episodes. In the plot, the 'black hole' Kugelblitz ended the universe and sent the remaining brothers from Umbrella Academy and Sparrow Academy to another reality. The problem is that their new versions don't have powers. The Umbrella Academy season 4 premiere has yet to be announced.
