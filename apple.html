Procon fines Apple R$ 12 million and already notifies about iPhone 14.
In the understanding of the consumer protection body, the practice is considered "tie sale", an abusive action, which forces the buyer to purchase the device, which is essential for the use of the device.
Maykon Lafaette 12/05/2019
New iPhone announced by Apple this weekend.
Procon Carioca fined Apple R$ 12.27 million for selling the iPhone 12 without a charger in the box. For the consumer protection agency, the company's practice is considered abusive, constituting a tie-in sale. Two other fines, also worth around R$12 million, may be applied, totaling around R$36 million. The agency sent the company, on Tuesday (23), two other notifications referring to cell phones of the iPhone 13 line and also about the iPhone 14, whose launch is scheduled for September 7.
The Procon gave a period of 20 days for the company to respond to the notification about the iPhone 13, which is part of a sanctioning process, as reported by the newspaper O Globo. In the case of the iPhone 14, which is not yet on the market, Procon aims at a preliminary investigation and determined a response period of five days. According to Igor Costa, executive director of Procon Carioca, Apple and other companies have already adopted this type of market practice for some time. For him, the charger is "an indispensable item for the regular functioning" of the smartphone. In the case of Apple, chargers are no longer shipped in boxes, in fact, from the iPhone 12 line onwards.
With the announcement of the new iphone, new photos were leaked.
"It is not new that, under the argument of sustainability, the largest companies in the telephone sector sell cell phones in Brazil by dividing their products, forcing consumers to purchase separately, an essential item for the regular operation of the same, the charger. We were looking at this in more detail and it is estimated that this practice generated an increase in revenue for Apple of more than US$ 6 billion", says Costa.
