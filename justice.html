Justice prohibits company from selling likes and followers on Instagram.
If you fail to comply with the order, Igoo Networks will have to pay a daily fine of R$10,000.
Maykon Lafaette 12/05/2019
Representation of Likes
The 1st Business Court and Arbitration Conflicts of São Paulo issued a decision yesterday (23) prohibiting the company Igoo Networks from selling likes and followers on Instagram. The company, which owns eight websites that sell engagement, was selling packages of 1,000 likes for R$0.60. According to Folha de São Paulo, the decision will take effect in a month and the defeated company will not be able to "develop, distribute, operate, sell or offer for sale services, products or applications that integrate with 'Instagram'".
The court also prohibited Igoo Networks from promoting or reproducing symbols associated with the social network, as some of the Brazilian company's websites make direct reference to the platform. The definition of the 1st Business Court and Arbitration Conflicts of SP met a request from Meta itself, the holding company that owns Instagram, which has sued Brazilian companies. Judge Luis Felipe Ferrari Bedendi ruled that Igoo Networks is violating Instagram's intellectual property and that it could only sell engagement if authorized.
Meta also said the company violated copyright law.
In the text, Judge Luis Felipe Ferrari Bedendi identified elements that authorize him to grant the injunctive relief requested by Meta, giving Igoo Networks 30 days to refrain from: Develop, distribute, operate, sell or offer for sale services, products or applications that integrate with Instagram; and Use, license or promote LIKESNOINSTA, FOLLOWERSGRAM, or any other signs that reproduce or imitate, in whole or in part, the trademarks registered by the author. In case of non-compliance, Igoo Networks will be penalized with a daily fine of R$ 10 thousand. "I was reading the news and I couldn't believe that a matter like this has actually been brought to justice." Said one user in an Instagram post.
