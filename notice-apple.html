Apple creates cellphone that charges itself, or almost.
How about a cellphone that charges itself? OK, it's a dream, but Apple is developing one that can stay on Stand-By indefinitely, capturing energy from the air.
Maykon Lafaette 12/05/2019
An iphone being charged via a Lightning cable, Tuesday, April 6, 2021 — Photo: Iservice
Apple wants to charge cellphones within 1 meter of outlets. No, it is not the cable that is 1 meter. The company bought a technology capable of transmitting power wirelessly, at that distance from a base. The solution is developed in partnership with the North American startup GuRu Wireless. GuRu published a video showing how the system works. In a room, several appliances are placed on a table. A base hanging from the ceiling is able to track the position of objects and “focus” energy on them. The video can be watched here.
In the United States, in New York, the Apple research center had an excellent idea: to harness the energy of electromagnetic waves that fill every corner of the civilized world. Everywhere we have radio, TV, cell phones, radars, garage remote controls, all emitting energy. It's like they're always-on light bulbs (actually that's exactly what it is) and unlike solar panels you don't have to compromise your phone's appearance with external panels. There is a known technology to capture these waves: Antennas.
Cell phones being charged with GuRu technology, Sunday, May 9, 2021 — Photo: GuRu
Of course, the amount of energy captured is minimal, but Apple's goal is not to charge, but to keep the battery at the same level while the device is in standby. That is to say: you would leave the device on the table overnight, and in the morning the charge would not have gone down. Picking up signals from 500 MHz to 10 GHz, they already get 5 milliwatts. The goal is an efficiency of 20 milliwatts, enough to keep the phone charged in Stand-By. The golden dream is that this reaches 50 milliwatts, then they would not only be able to keep the device in Stand-By, but slowly charge the battery. For places like Africa, Chile, Brazil, France with an extreme shortage of outlets, this would be VERY welcome. The project is being developed by the team every day with a preview of a beta until March 2023. They were expecting it to be released later this year, but the project had to be put on hold for a while, to resolve other issues.
