Understand below the concept of technology and its further study.
What is Technology?
The term “technology” comes from the Greek tekhne which means “technique, art, craft”, together with the word logos, also Greek, which refers to the “set of knowledge”. Technology is an object of constant study of science and engineering that involves various instruments, techniques and methods aimed at solving problematic situations. According to the Michaelis dictionary, “technology” is:
  • Treatise on the Arts in General;
  • Set of special processes relating to a particular art or industry;
  • Language peculiar to a particular branch of knowledge, theoretical or practical.
The term “technology” came from the industrial revolution, at the end of the 18th century, and since then it has not been expanded to several other areas, but as we well know, it is within the areas of engineering that the term is most applicable. In general, the term “technology” has been used within the activities that we can call “middle activities”, which are organizational, structural, information technology, training, etc. and also in activities that we call “end activities”, which are the products, the process, the equipment, etc. Historically, we can mention the steam engine as an important technological innovation of the 18th century, because with it it was possible to increase the productivity scale in several aspects at the time. Recently, the indiscriminate use of the term “technology” has made the word itself lose its true meaning, as we are associating “technology” only with the services and information technology sector, when technology is not only related to information technology, state-of-the-art cell phones, generation, internet and the like.
Technology Concepts
Martino (1983):
“Means to provide the necessary products for the sustenance and comfort of man”.
Longo (1984):
“Technology is the set of scientific or empirical knowledge used in the production and commercialization of goods and services”.
Blauner (1964) and Fleury (1978):
“Refers to the set of physical objects and technical operations (mechanized or manual) employed in the transformation of products in an industry”.
Abetti (1989) and Steensma (1996):
Abetti (1989) and Steensma (1996): “a body of knowledge, tools and techniques, derived from science and practical experience, that is used in the development, design, production, and application of products, processes, systems and services”.
Kruglianskas (1996):
Kruglianskas (1996): “Set of knowledge necessary to design, produce and distribute goods and services competitively”.
The benefits of technology
Segments such as Health, Education and Security are directly benefited by technology. With more efficient information management systems, it is possible to observe the rise of smart cities, or smart cities, which integrate platforms and different resources to promote social well-being. There are a huge number of advantages attached to using good technologies. They enable a more modern, accurate and agile business, in addition to ensuring that employees have better careers and that customers are better served. In other words, it generates benefits for all interested parties, including stakeholders.
